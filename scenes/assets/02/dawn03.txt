You turn over in the dirt, reaching for something, 
anything, but you flounder in the dim glow. 
Suddenly, you gaze ahead, distracted. Beyond a 
small clearing extends, in all directions, an 
immense meadow of wild grass and flowers. No field 
could ever grow so perfectly even, unbroken, and 
endless. But it had, and along its boundary, ran a 
hedge of equal wonder. It stands now at the end of 
vision, tall and tangled.
