To be born with the spring, to die with the roses,  
To float on the zephyr's wing in a pure sky,   
To sway in the heart of flowers scarcely opened,  
Drunk with perfumes, on light and azure,      
Shaking, still young, the dust from her wings,  
To fly away like a breath into the eternal vault,  
Here is the butterfly's enchanting destiny!  
How like desire, which never comes to rest,  
And never satisfied, brushing against everything,  
Returns at last to heaven in search of delight!
